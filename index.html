I am a PhD student in Computer Science and NSF Graduate Research Fellow at Cornell Tech. My research investigates machine learning and causal inference methods for computational biomedicine.

I am advised by Dr. Fei Wang, Dr. Volodymyr Kuleshov, and Dr. Kyra Gan. I collaborate with the Weill Cornell Medicine Institute of AI for Digital Health. The first year of my PhD was internally funded by the Presidential Life Science Fellowship. Formally, my major concentrations are artificial intelligence and scientific computing. My minor concentration is in applied probability and statistics. In summer 2022, I was a Clinical Data Science Intern at Boehringer Ingelheim investigating deep learning methods for multi-modal survival analysis.

       

CURRENT WORK

My work emphasizes computational methods that automate and accelerate clinical research and drug development. I am particularly interested in probabilistic, generative, and latent variable models; leveraging probabilistic graphical models for causal insights; and the challenges that arise in low-data settings.

A subset of my publications are highlighted below. The rest of my publications can be accessed through Google Scholar.

Regularized Data Programming with Automated Bayesian Prior Selection. Workshop paper: ICML Workshop on Structured Probabilistic Inference & Generative Modeling, 2023. J Maasch, H Zhang, Q Yang, F Wang, V Kuleshov.

Automated data labeling improves with Bayesian regularization in low-data regimes. A straightforward data-driven procedure simplifies prior parameter selection.

Molecular de-extinction of ancient antimicrobial peptides enabled by machine learning. Forthcoming: Cell Host & Microbe, 2023. J Maasch*, M Torres*, M Melo, C de la Fuente. *Equal contribution.

Machine learning guides bioinspired prospection for encrypted antimicrobial peptides that display in vitro and in vivo activity with low host toxicity.

PRIOR WORK

As a master's student researcher at Penn Engineering, I used machine learning to investigate the physicochemical properties and therapeutic potential of antimicrobial peptides. R PACKAGES

I make R packages in my spare time, mostly for exploring color theory in data visualization. All packages are on GitHub.

sanzo: R Color Palettes Based on the Works of Sanzo Wada ‚Äì A CRAN Package.

Inspired by the art and color research of designer Sanzo Wada (1883-1967), his Dictionary Of Color Combinations, and the incredible interactive site by Dain M. Blodorn Kim, this package brings Wada's color combinations to R for easy use in data visualizations. This package honors 60 of Wada's color combinations: 20 duos, 20 trios, and 20 quads.
